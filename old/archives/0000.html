Internet Freedom

Written by Chiayo Lin | JUN 10, 2014 | Saigon, Vietnam


"We should keep the Internet an open network where every individual is free to connect."

What is Internet?


The Internet is a computer network consisting of a worldwide network of computer networks that use the TCP/IP network protocols to facilitate data transmission and exchange[1]. Basically,every user on the Internet is a node, without a central governing system.


Introduction


Internet freedom is considered a basic human right by the United Nations [2]. Therefore, every human has the right to access the Internet freely. However, some governments are taking away their people's ability to access the Internet by creating unreasonable policies, acts, or agreements, such as, SOPA, TPP, and Golden Shield Project.
The following four principles are the core ideas of Internet freedom:

  • Governments do not have the right to censor the Internet.
  • Keep the Internet an open network where everyone is free to connect.
  • Protect the freedom to innovate and create without permission.
  • Protect privacy and defend everyone’s ability to control how their data and devices are used.

Why is Internet freedom considered a basic human right?


Internet freedom should be considered a basic right because, according to the U.N. Human Rights Council, everybody has the right to access information and communicate freely, whether they are online or offline [3].


SOPA ↑


SOPA (Stop Online Piracy Act) would allow the US government to devise a list of websites that US citizens are not allowed to visit [4]. SOPA is an online censorship program in North America, just like a Chinese-style firewall.

If we lost the ability to communicate with each other over the internet, it would be a change of Bill of Rights...... - Aaron Swartz [5-1]

This Act allows the US government to shutdown websites whenever they think there is any "anti-copyright" activity. The US government would mainly be targeting Torrent servers [6] and sites such as Wikileaks[7]. This would be a violation of Internet freedom because it stops humans from connecting with certain groups on Internet.

New technology should bring us greater freedom...... - Aaron Swartz [5-2]

TPP ↑


TPP (Trans-Pacific Partnership Agreement) is a trade agreement across 12 countries: Brunei, Chile, New Zealand, Singapore, United States, Australia, Peru, Vietnam, Malaysia, Mexico, Canada, and Japan [8].

Under this TPP proposal, ISPs (Internet Service Providers) could be required to "police" user activity (i.e. police YOU), take down internet content, and cut people off from internet access for common user-generated content [9].
Breaking digital locks (DRM) for legit purposes, such as using Linux, could subject users to mandatory fines [10].

The TPP agreement is violation of Internet freedom because it doesn't protect the freedom to innovate and limits the freedom of expression. It also decreases the ability to connect with people over the Internet because it allows ISPs to supervise you and cut you from the Internet. This is violating one of the core ideas of internet freedom, "Keep the Internet an open network where everyone is free to connect."


Golden Shield Project ↑


The Golden Shield Project is an Internet censorship and surveillance project that has been operated by the Chinese government since 2003 [11-1].

Ultimately, the aim is to integrate a gigantic online database with an all-encompassing surveillance network – incorporating speech and face recognition, closed-circuit television, smart cards, credit records, and Internet surveillance technologies [11-2].

Some methods that Golden Shield Project is using to censor the Internet are [11-3]:


Method Description
IP blocking The access to a certain IP address is denied.
DNS filtering & redirection This affects all IP protocols such as HTTP, FTP or POP.
URL filtering This affects the Hypertext Transfer Protocol.
Packet filtering Terminate TCP packet transmissions when a certain number of controversial keywords are detected.

The Golden Shield Project has violated Internet freedom because it violates all the rights that are guaranteed by Internet freedom. The Golden Shield Project allows the Chinese government to censor the Intrnet, close off the Internet, limit innovation, and control its citizens' data and devices.
